Room Organizer

A simple planning tool for people living in small or shared spaces. This app is designed to help students, renters, and young adults think through how their room can support their daily life — not just fit furniture.

Instead of focusing on perfect measurements or professional design, Room Organizer encourages realistic choices based on what you already own, what you value most, and the limits of your space.

Room Profile

Start by describing the physical limits of your room. Many people struggle with layouts because they underestimate how quickly furniture fills a small space.

This section helps you think about your room as a container with real boundaries.





Furniture Inventory

People often buy furniture before thinking about how it fits with what they already own. This section flips that process.

Listing your furniture helps prevent overcrowding and unnecessary purchases.





Style & Use Preferences

Small spaces rarely support everything equally. Choosing a main priority helps users make intentional trade-offs.

This reflects how people actually live — some need storage, others need focus, and others want a space to socialize.

Layout Suggestions

Based on the room size, furniture list, and priorities, the app would suggest general layout strategies.

Shopping List

This section highlights missing or helpful items, such as compact storage, multi-use furniture, or alternatives to oversized pieces.

The goal is to support mindful purchasing instead of impulse buying.

3D Model / Visual Preview

A simple visual representation helps users understand scale and spacing in a way numbers alone cannot.

This feature is meant to reduce anxiety and increase confidence before rearranging or purchasing furniture.
